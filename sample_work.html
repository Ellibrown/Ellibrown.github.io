I am currently working on a project that investigates the relationship between walkable urban design and community well-being. My project compares a large dataset of walkability scores of cities with another dataset of happiness scores of cities to see if there is a relationship between the two factors.

This has been my research methodology for this project:

  1. Define project goals and hypotheses
  2. Gather and evaluate data sources
  3. Clean data
  4. Data analysis and visualization
  5. Report and communicate findings
  6. Reflect on starting goals and hypotheses

Here is a sample of some of the data I have used in this project:

City Walkability Score Happiness Score
Mexico City 0.18/1.0 6.69/10
Helsinki 0.851/1.0 7.83/10

I sourced the happiness index scores from the World Happiness Report and the walkability scores from Pedestrians First.
